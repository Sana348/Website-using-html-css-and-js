Self-Affirmation Theory takes this one step further by suggesting that when our self-esteem is threatened, we sometimes affirm other parts of ourselves unrelated to the threat or do things that make us feel good about other aspects of ourselves to protect our self-esteem. When we do this, it helps us realize that our self-worth is not contingent on whatever negative feedback or experience we're having right now. As a result, we might be less likely to be defensive, distort information to fit our worldview, and we can better tolerate threats to our self-esteem
